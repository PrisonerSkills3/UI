Benefits to Hiring a Worker with a Criminal Background:

A federal Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC) is available to employers who hire a person convicted of a felony within a year of the conviction or the date of release from prison. The tax credit often is $2400.

The Department of Labor offers Free Bonding to Promote Hiring of Workers with Criminal Records. The bond protects an employer against losses related to potential employee theft or dishonesty.

How to talk about Conviction:

Be honest. Never lie to an interviewer or put false information on your resume or application. This will disqualify you when the employer does a background check or checks your references.

Keep it simple. Answer questions directly. Address any concerns an employer might have, then steer the interview back to your skills and the positive traits that you bring to the job.

Make a good first and last impression. If possible, try to address your criminal history in the middle of the interview. End with a summary of your qualifications and interest in the job. Emphasize the positive.
